    Diet Guidelines Before PET CT: What You Should Eat and What to Avoid

    PET CT dos and donts

    Proper diet plays a very drastic role in getting clear and accurate PET CT scan results. The goal is to keep your blood sugar stable and reduce muscle activity so the radioactive tracer can target the right areas. Follow these diet rules strictly for 24 hours before your scan unless your doctor advises otherwise.

    What You Should Eat Before a PET CT Scan?

    Choose foods that are low-carbohydrate, high-protein, and low-sugar. These help stabilize glucose levels and improve tracer uptake.

    Allowed foods:

    • Plain vegetables (boiled or raw; non-starchy)
    • Green leafy vegetables
    • Paneer (cottage cheese)
    • Tofu / Soya
    • Eggs (if you eat them)
    • Lentils and dals in limited quantity
    • Moong dal cheela without sugar
    • Plain curd (unsweetened)
    • Nuts (almonds, walnuts; small quantity)
    • Grilled or steamed foods
    • Clear soups
    • Plenty of plain water
    • Tea/Coffee Allowed only without sugar and without milk.

    What You Must Avoid Before a PET CT Scan?

    Avoid anything that causes a spike in blood sugar or activates muscles.

    Do not consume:

    • All fruits (even banana, apple, mango, etc.)
    • All fruit juices
    • All sugary foods (sweets, chocolates, desserts)
    • All dairy with sugar or flavouring
    • Milk and milk-based drinks
    • Rice, chapati, bread, pasta, noodles
    • Potatoes, sweet potatoes, corn (starchy vegetables)
    • Cold drinks, energy drinks, packaged juices
    • Alcohol
    • Fast food / fried food
    • Tea/coffee with milk or sugar
    • Protein shakes, health supplements
    • Any glucose-based solution

    Additional Important Instructions

    • No eating for 6 hours before the scan (complete fasting).
    • Water is allowed, and staying hydrated is recommended.
    • Diabetic patients must follow a modified prep as per doctor instructions (I can write that section too if needed).

    After Your PET CT Scan: Signs That You Should Call Your Doctor

    Most patients feel completely normal after a PET CT scan and can return to their routine within a few hours. The radioactive tracer leaves the body naturally through urine, and side effects are extremely rare.
    However, it is important to stay alert to any unusual symptoms and know when you should contact your doctor.

    Normal After-Scan Effects (No Need to Worry)

    Best PET CT centre in Chandigarh

    • These mild symptoms are common and usually settle on their own:
    • Feeling tired for a few hours
    • Increased urination (because your body is flushing out the tracer)
    • Mild soreness at the injection site
    • If these symptoms improve with rest and hydration, they are considered normal.

    When You Should Contact Your Doctor

    PET CT scan near me

    Reach out to your healthcare provider immediately if you notice any of the following:

    1. Persistent or Worsening Pain at the Injection Site

    Increasing redness

    Swelling or warmth

    Formation of a hard lump
    These may indicate an infection or allergic reaction.

    1. Rash, Itching, or Breathing Difficulty

    Though rare, these may be signs of an allergic response to the injected tracer.

    1. Fever or Chills

    A temperature above 100.4F (38C) within 2448 hours of the scan should be reported.

    1. Severe Nausea, Vomiting, or Dizziness

    This is uncommon and may require medical attention.

    1. If You Are Pregnant or Breastfeeding

    Contact your doctor if:

    You accidentally had close contact with infants or pregnant women within 24 hours

    You feel unsure about when it is safe to resume breastfeeding

    1. Any Unusual Symptom That Persists Beyond 24 Hours

    If something doesnt feel normal or is getting worse, its better to consult your doctor.

    Emergency Warning Signs (Seek Help Immediately)

    • Call emergency services if you experience:
    • Difficulty breathing
    • Chest pain
    • Severe allergic reaction
      These are extremely rare but require urgent care.

    How long does a PET CT scan take?

    A PET CT scan generally takes 2 to 3 hours from start to finish, although the actual scanning time is much shorter.

    1. Preparation Time

    Approximately 60 to 90 minutes.
    After you arrive, a small amount of radioactive tracer is injected. You will need to rest quietly while the tracer circulates through your body. This is the longest part of the appointment.

    1. Scanning Time

    Approximately 15 to 25 minutes.
    You will lie still on the scanner bed while the PET and CT images are captured. The scan itself is painless.

    1. Post-Scan Observation

    Approximately 5 to 10 minutes.
    You may be advised to drink water and wait briefly before leaving the centre.

    Total Time Required: 2 to 3 Hours

    If the centre is busy or a whole-body detailed scan is required, the timing may vary slightly.

    Various Utilities of PET/CT

    PET CT Scan with Various Utilities

    Diagnosis

    PET/CT helps determine whether a lesion is benign or malignant by analyzing cell activity inside the body. Moreover, when MRI or CT cannot confirm the nature of a lesion, PET-CT imaging provides clear and early diagnostic information. Therefore, doctors gain confidence in planning timely and accurate treatment.

    Staging

    After a confirmed diagnosis, PET/CT identifies the exact spread of disease before treatment begins. Additionally, accurate staging with PET CT examination ensures the most appropriate and targeted treatment plan for patients. Therefore, doctors can choose the best course of treatment right from the start.

    Response Evaluation

    PET Scan evaluates how well the treatment is working, either during or after therapy. Moreover, PET nuclear scan helps doctors decide whether to continue, modify, or stop a specific treatment. Therefore, patients receive the right care at the right time, ensuring no time or effort is wasted.

    Restaging

    PET/CT checks the present condition of the disease after treatment. Furthermore, it supports doctors in planning the most suitable further treatment based on accurate updated findings. Consequently, patients continue with the most effective care pathway.

    Suspected Recurrence

    When symptoms or lab results raise concern and other tests appear normal, a PET Scan can, however, detect early recurrence. Moreover, it helps identify disease activity at a very early stage. Consequently, doctors are able to take timely and targeted action to manage the condition more effectively.

    Follow-Up / Surveillance

    After treatment success, PET Scan is used for routine monitoring to ensure the disease has not returned. Moreover, regular PET/CT surveillance gives patients and doctors assurance of ongoing good health. Therefore, continuous follow-up helps maintain long-term wellness.
